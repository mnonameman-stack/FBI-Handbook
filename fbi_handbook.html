Section 01

Director's Directive

To all Bureau Personnel —
The Federal Bureau of Investigation stands as a pillar of order, justice, and unwavering professionalism. This handbook is not a suggestion — it is a binding contract between you and the Bureau. Every agent, from the newest trainee at Quantico to the most seasoned Senior Supervisory veteran, is held to these standards without exception.

Respect the badge. Respect the mission. Follow the protocol.

This document governs conduct, communications, operational behavior, and the chain of command across all Bureau channels and roleplay environments. Failure to adhere to the standards outlined herein will result in disciplinary action commensurate with the severity of the violation. Ignorance of these guidelines is not a defense — all agents are expected to have read and understood this handbook in full prior to their first field deployment.

All members are required to acknowledge receipt of this handbook. Participation in any Bureau activity constitutes acceptance of these terms.

Section 02

Code of Conduct Enforced

The following directives constitute the foundational behavioral standards of the Bureau. Compliance is mandatory for all personnel regardless of rank, tenure, or assignment. These rules apply across all Bureau channels, voice communications, and roleplay scenarios.

DIRECTIVE 01
Show Respect at All Times
Treat all members regardless of rank with professionalism and courtesy. Disrespect, harassment, and trolling will not be tolerated under any circumstances. The Bureau's reputation begins with how its agents treat one another.
DIRECTIVE 02
Use Proper Channels
Keep all discussions relevant to their designated channels. Avoid spamming, flooding, or repurposing channels for off-topic content. Every channel has a purpose — respect that structure.
DIRECTIVE 03
Follow the Chain of Command
Report issues, concerns, or grievances to your direct superior only. Bypassing the chain of command disrupts Bureau operations and will be treated as insubordination.
DIRECTIVE 04
Maintain Roleplay Integrity
Keep In-Character (IC) and Out-of-Character (OOC) behavior strictly separate. Metagaming, powergaming, and breaking immersion are prohibited. The integrity of the operation depends on your discipline.
DIRECTIVE 05
Protect Sensitive Information
Do not discuss active operations or confidential Bureau matters in public channels. What happens in the Bureau stays in the Bureau. Unauthorized disclosure of operational intelligence is a serious offense.
DIRECTIVE 06
Post Appropriate Content Only
No NSFW, copyrighted, or offensive material of any kind. Profile pictures, nicknames, and usernames must remain professional and consistent with Bureau standards at all times.
DIRECTIVE 07
Communicate Professionally
Use proper grammar, avoid excessive pinging, and keep messages clear and purposeful. Every message you send reflects on the Bureau. Write accordingly — you represent the Federal Government.
DIRECTIVE 08
Complete Required Training
All new members must pass mandatory training to earn full operational status. No exceptions. No shortcuts. Training exists to protect both the agent and the Bureau's mission.
DIRECTIVE 09
Exercise Common Sense
If an action is clearly disruptive, disrespectful, or harmful — even if not explicitly listed here — it is against the rules. The spirit of this handbook applies broadly. Ignorance is not immunity.
DIRECTIVE 10
Maintain Operational Confidentiality
Never share screenshots, logs, or details of internal Bureau discussions with outside parties. Classified information stays classified. Leaking internal Bureau communications is grounds for immediate removal.
DIRECTIVE 11
Represent the Bureau Professionally
Your conduct outside Bureau channels still reflects on the organization. Agents are always on the clock. Do not engage in behavior elsewhere that would bring disrepute to the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
DIRECTIVE 12
Support Fellow Agents
The Bureau operates as a unit. Report suspicious behavior, offer assistance to fellow agents, and foster a collaborative environment. Division within the ranks weakens the entire organization.

Section 03

Disciplinary Framework

Violations of Bureau directives are addressed proportionally based on severity and pattern of behavior. All disciplinary actions are logged in the Bureau's internal record system. Repeated infractions of any tier escalate automatically to the next tier regardless of individual severity.

Tier Severity Typical Violations Action
TIER I LOW Minor spam, OOC in IC channel, mild disrespect, excessive pinging Verbal warning / Formal written warning
TIER II MODERATE Repeated Tier I offenses, sustained harassment, bypassing chain of command, inappropriate content Temporary mute (1–24 hrs)
TIER III HIGH Metagaming, disclosing operational intel, sustained disrespect, failure to comply with superior orders Extended mute or server kick
TIER IV CRITICAL Doxxing, NSFW content, deliberate sabotage of operations, impersonating command staff, information leaks Permanent ban — no appeal
All disciplinary decisions are at the discretion of Supervisory personnel and Bureau Command. The Bureau reserves the right to skip tiers for exceptionally severe single violations.

Section 04

Roleplay Protocols

Maintaining immersion and fairness during operations is essential to the Bureau's identity. The following protocols govern how roleplay is to be conducted across all scenarios, from routine patrols to high-stakes investigations.

🔵
IC / OOC Separation
Always clearly distinguish between In-Character and Out-of-Character speech. Use designated OOC channels or brackets like (( )) for OOC communication during active RP. Never break immersion mid-scene without cause.
🚫
No Metagaming
Using out-of-character knowledge to influence in-character decisions is strictly prohibited. Your character knows only what they have discovered IC. What you see in a Discord channel does not exist in the field.
⚖️
No Powergaming
Do not perform actions your character could not realistically accomplish. Do not control another player's character without their consent. Roleplay is collaborative — not competitive. All parties must have meaningful agency.
📋
Operation Briefings
All field operations require a briefing issued through proper command channels before deployment. Agents must not act outside the operational scope defined in their briefing documents. Mission creep is a disciplinary offense.
🗂️
Evidence & Documentation
All evidence gathered during investigations must be logged and submitted to the appropriate supervisory agent. Tampering with, destroying, or failing to report evidence is grounds for immediate review.
🛑
Pause Protocol
If an OOC issue arises during active RP, any agent may call a pause using the designated signal. All roleplay halts until the matter is resolved. Ignoring a pause call is a disciplinary offense.
🔏
Confidential Operations
Operations classified above your clearance level are strictly off-limits. Do not inquire about, speculate on, or attempt to access classified operational files without authorization from Command.
👔
Character Conduct
Your character must behave in a manner consistent with a federal law enforcement officer. Gratuitous brutality, corruption roleplay, or rogue agent behavior requires prior Command approval and must serve a narrative purpose.
📡
Communication Standards
Use proper radio and communications etiquette at all times. Channels have assigned frequencies — use them correctly. Transmissions should be clear, brief, and professional. No personal conversations over operational comms.

Section 06

Bureau Glossary

Standard terminology used across all Bureau communications and roleplay environments.

IC
In-Character — actions, dialogue, and behavior occurring within the roleplay scenario as your agent character.
OOC
Out-of-Character — communication occurring outside the roleplay scenario, as the real person behind the character.
Metagaming
Using knowledge your character could not realistically possess (typically OOC information) to influence IC decisions. Strictly prohibited.
Powergaming
Performing actions your character could not realistically accomplish, or controlling another player's character without consent.
SAC
Special Agent in Charge — the commanding officer of a field office.
ASAC
Assistant Special Agent in Charge — the deputy commander of a field division.
ADIC
Assistant Director in Charge — commands one of the three largest U.S. field offices.
EAD
Executive Assistant Director — manages a principal functional branch at FBI Headquarters.
SES
Senior Executive Service — the highest civil service classification, covering FBI executive leadership.
GS Grade
General Schedule pay grade — the federal government classification system ranging GS-1 through GS-15, followed by SES and Executive levels.
Quantico
The FBI Academy in Quantico, Virginia — where all new agent trainees complete their mandatory basic training.
HUMINT
Human Intelligence — intelligence gathered through interpersonal contact and informant networks rather than technical surveillance.
Op-Sec
Operational Security — the practice of protecting sensitive operational information from unauthorized disclosure.

Section 07

The Agent's Oath

Upon completion of training and acceptance into the Bureau, all agents affirm the following oath. This commitment is not ceremonial — it defines the standard to which every member of this organization is held, every day, in every action.

"I do solemnly swear that I will uphold the honor and integrity of the Federal Bureau of Investigation — that I will serve the Bureau and its mission with Fidelity, Bravery, and Integrity; that I will respect the chain of command and the badge I carry; that I will safeguard the operations, the agents, and the mission entrusted to my care; and that I will hold myself to the highest standard of conduct, in the field, and in all matters of the Bureau, so help me."

— Affirmed upon induction into the Federal Bureau of Investigation

By participating in Bureau operations, all agents implicitly reaffirm this oath. Your badge is a privilege — protect it.
